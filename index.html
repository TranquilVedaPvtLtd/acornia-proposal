Why Generic CRMs Break for MFDs

Zoho Creator is an excellent general-purpose platform. It was never built to understand what a mutual fund distributor does on a Tuesday afternoon.

The 15-login problem: In a 5-person MFD firm where each person wears 3 roles (RM, Operations, Service RM), Zoho requires 15 separate logins. This isn't a configuration problem. It's a fundamental design mismatch — Zoho assumes one person does one job.
Pain 01

One RM cannot hold Service RM + Operations access simultaneously

Your team switches logins multiple times a day. Every switch is a context break, a delay, and a chance for something to slip.

This leads to slower client service and attrition tied to the person, not the firm
Pain 02

The software is passive. Your RMs are the system.

No pop-up when an SIP is about to lapse. No alert when a KYC expires. No guided step for a transaction type a junior RM has never processed before.

This leads to missed renewals, compliance gaps and the owner can never step back from operations
Pain 03

Investwell gives you reports. Not intelligence.

Your data contains every insight your business needs. None of it gets actioned because there's no layer between "report exists" and "RM does something about it."

This leads to reactive decisions and AUM attrition mistaken for market conditions
Pain 04

Data entry consumes the hours that should go to clients

Generic forms require manual AMFI/BSE data entry. No smart defaults. No auto-population. RMs spend 30-40% of working hours on admin.

This leads to RM burnout, incomplete records and BI built on data you can't trust
Pain 05

7 transaction types handled by 1 generic form

An SIP registration, a switch, a redemption, an STP — each has different compliance steps. Zoho's one-size form verifies none of them.

This leads to compliance gaps and the owner discovers missed steps months later
Pain 06

If your senior RM leaves, so does your client's history

No cross-portfolio visibility. When a client calls and their RM is unavailable, the next person has no context — no recent conversations, no pending transactions.

This leads to client service gaps and the business being worth less than its AUM
Role Architecture

One login. Every hat.

Built around how lean MFD teams actually work — not how enterprise software thinks they should.

  • Priya logs in once. She sees her RM task queue, the Ops pending approvals, and the client portfolios she services — all in one view.
  • Permissions flex by context, not by label. An Admin can narrow their view to act as an RM when needed. No system administrator needed.
  • New RMs onboard in days, not months. The system shows them what to do next at every step. No tribal knowledge required.
  • Cross-portfolio visibility — firm-level, not RM-level. When a client calls, any authorized team member can see their full history.
