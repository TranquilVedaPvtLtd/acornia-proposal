Why Generic CRMs Break for MFDs

Zoho Creator is an excellent general-purpose platform. It was never built to understand what a mutual fund distributor does on a Tuesday afternoon.

The 15-login problem: In a 5-person MFD firm where each person wears 3 roles (RM, Operations, Service RM), Zoho requires 15 separate logins to give everyone the access they need. This isn't a configuration problem. It's a fundamental design mismatch — Zoho assumes one person does one job.
Pain 01

One RM cannot hold Service RM + Operations access simultaneously

Your team switches logins multiple times a day. Every switch is a context break, a delay, and a chance for something to slip. In a small firm, the person answering a client's call is also the person processing their transaction.

This leads to → slower client service → client frustration → attrition tied to the person, not the firm
Pain 02

The software is passive. Your RMs are the system.

No pop-up when an SIP is about to lapse. No alert when a KYC expires. No guided step for a transaction type a junior RM has never processed before. Everything that doesn't get remembered, doesn't get done.

This leads to → missed renewals, compliance gaps → owner audits manually → owner can never step back from operations
Pain 03

Investwell gives you reports. Not intelligence.

Your Investwell data contains every insight your business needs — which clients are at risk, which SIPs are stagnant, which portfolios have drifted. None of it gets actioned because there's no layer between "report exists" and "RM does something about it."

This leads to → reactive decisions → owner manually cross-references PDFs → AUM attrition mistaken for market conditions
Pain 04

Data entry consumes the hours that should go to clients

Generic forms require manual AMFI/BSE data entry. No smart defaults. No auto-population. RMs spend 30–40% of working hours on admin — not because they want to, but because the software makes every step a manual step.

This leads to → RM burnout, incomplete records → BI built on bad data → you make decisions on data you can't trust
Pain 05

7 transaction types handled by 1 generic form

An SIP registration, a switch, a redemption, an STP — each has different compliance steps, different validations, different risk flags. Zoho's one-size form verifies none of them. Compliance is whatever the RM remembers, not what the system enforces.

This leads to → compliance gaps → audit risk → owner discovers missed steps months later, not days
Pain 06

If your senior RM leaves, so does your client's history

No cross-portfolio visibility. When a client calls and their RM is unavailable, the next person has no context — no recent conversations, no pending transactions, no prior instructions. The firm's relationship lives in one person's memory, not the system.

This leads to → client service gaps → the business is worth less than its AUM because the knowledge is non-transferable
Role Architecture

One login. Every hat.

The platform is built around how lean MFD teams actually work — not how enterprise software thinks they should. A person has access to everything they need for their role at any given moment, in a single session.

  • Priya logs in once. She sees her RM task queue, the Ops pending approvals she's responsible for, and the client portfolios she services on behalf of another RM — all in one view, with no login switching.
  • Permissions flex by context, not by label. An Admin can narrow their view to act as an RM when needed. An Ops person handling a specific portfolio can see just that context. No system administrator needed.
  • New RMs onboard in days, not months. The system shows them what to do next at every step. No tribal knowledge. No manual training on which fields matter for which transaction type.
  • Cross-portfolio visibility — firm-level, not RM-level. When a client calls, any authorized team member can see their full history, pending tasks, and last conversation — not just their assigned RM.
